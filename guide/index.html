Our Guide

Recommendations

1. Start planning early: Summer camps in the USA can be very popular, so it's important to start planning as soon as possible. This will give you plenty of time to research different options, apply for visas if necessary, and make travel arrangements.

2. Choose the right camp:There are many different types of summer camps in the USA, so it's important to choose one that meets your needs and interests. Consider factors such as location, activities, and language programs when making your decision.

3. Pack appropriately: Make sure to pack clothes and other items that are appropriate for the climate and activities at your camp. You'll likely need comfortable shoes, sunscreen, and a water bottle, among other things.

4. Bring a camera: You'll want to capture all of the amazing experiences you'll have at camp, so make sure to bring a camera or smartphone to take pictures and videos.

5. Learn about American culture: Summer camps in the USA are a great opportunity to learn about American culture, so take advantage of this by exploring local attractions and trying new foods.

6. Make friends: One of the best parts of attending a summer camp is meeting new people from all over the world. Be open to making new friends and participating in group activities.

7. Practice your language skills: Summer camps in the USA often offer language classes and other opportunities to practice your language skills. Make the most of these by actively participating and speaking with other students.

8. Research your destination: Before you arrive, learn as much as you can about the area where your summer camp is located. This will help you to get a better sense of the local culture and what to expect during your stay.

9. Get your paperwork in order: Make sure you have all of the necessary paperwork for your trip, including your passport, visa, and travel insurance.

10. Bring a refillable water bottle: Staying hydrated is important, and a refillable water bottle can save you money and reduce waste.

11. Be open-minded: Embrace new experiences and don't be afraid to step outside of your comfort zone.

12. Stay organized: Keep track of important documents and information, such as your schedule, contact information, and emergency numbers.

13. Be respectful: Show respect for the local culture and customs, and be mindful of your behavior and actions.

14. Get plenty of rest: With so many activities and new experiences, it can be tempting to stay up late every night. However, getting enough sleep is essential for staying healthy and energized.

15. Bring a journal: Capture your memories and experiences by keeping a journal or blog during your trip.

16. Be mindful of your budget: Make a budget for your trip and stick to it, as unexpected expenses can quickly add up.

17. Stay safe: Always be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe, such as traveling in groups and avoiding isolated areas.

18. Have fun: Above all, remember to have fun and enjoy the unique and unforgettable experiences that a summer camp in the USA has to offer!
